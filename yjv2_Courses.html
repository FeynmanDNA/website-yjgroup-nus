Courses

The ongoing module Prof Yan Jie has been teaching for the past several years is Advanced Biophysics offered by Department of Physics NUS.

PC5213 ADVANCED BIOPHYSICS

Learning Outcomes

This module discusses the molecules and machines in cells and their mechanisms. It focuses on theories and techniques used in some important areas of biophysics and life sciences. The general topics and the useful techniques are to be introduced in the first half of the semester. In the second half of the semester, the topics will be emphasized on the structures and transitions of macromolecules, with a focus on the nucleic acids. The students are to learn the most recent, exciting development of the micromechanics of DNA molecules.

Several important experimental techniques are to be introduced. They are: 1) X-Ray diffraction, 2) Nuclear Magnetic Resonance, 3) Scanning Tunnelling Microscopy, 4) Single-molecule manipulation tweezers, 5) Patch clamping, and their applications to the study of biomacromolecules.

This module also includes some lab components. The students are expected to study the DNA micromechanics by imaging DNA molecules relaxed in 2D using AFM. This module is targeted at both physics and non-physics students who already have learnt statistical physics, and have basic knowledge of molecular biology.

Prerequisites

Prerequisites: Statistical physics, and either PC4267 or PC4268, or Departmental Approval.

Projects

  • Study the DNA elasticity by 2D imaging of DNA using AFM
  • Measurement of DNA bending rigidity using a magnetic tweezers
  • Monte Carlo simulation studies of DNA conformations
